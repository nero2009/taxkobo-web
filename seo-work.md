### Influencer-Specific FAQ (Add to Website)

**New FAQ questions to add:**

**6. "Do I need to pay tax on YouTube earnings in Nigeria?"**
> **Answer:** Yes! YouTube AdSense payments (received in USD) are taxable income in Nigeria under the Personal Income Tax Act. You must convert your YouTube earnings to Naira using the official CBN exchange rate at the time of each payment and include the total in your annual PIT filing by March 31st. Even if you haven't withdrawn the money to your Nigerian bank account, it's still taxable income.

**7. "What about Instagram/TikTok brand deals and sponsorships?"**
> **Answer:** All brand deals, sponsored posts, and paid partnerships are fully taxable income, whether paid in Naira, USD, EUR, or any other currency. This includes product gifting if it has monetary value (e.g., brand sends you ‚Ç¶200k phone = ‚Ç¶200k taxable income). You must track all payments and file PIT on your total annual income.

**8. "I'm only a small influencer with 10k followers. Do I still need to file taxes?"**
> **Answer:** If your total annual income from ALL sources (brand deals, AdSense, affiliate marketing, day job, etc.) is above ‚Ç¶300,000, you are required to file PIT in Nigeria. The number of followers doesn't matter - only your total income. Even small influencers earning ‚Ç¶1M+ per year must file by March 31st to avoid penalties.

**9. "What is the VAT threshold for influencers?"**
> **Answer:** If your annual turnover (total income before expenses) exceeds ‚Ç¶25 million, you must register for VAT with FIRS. Many successful influencers (50k+ subscribers, regular brand deals) are above this threshold and don't realize it. TaxKobo's calculator will alert you if you're approaching or above the VAT threshold.

**10. "Can I deduct my camera, ring light, and editing software?"**
> **Answer:** Yes! Business expenses like camera equipment, lighting, microphones, editing software (Adobe, Final Cut Pro), laptop, and even a portion of your internet/electricity bills are tax-deductible for content creators. Our full TaxKobo platform helps you track these deductions throughout the year.
